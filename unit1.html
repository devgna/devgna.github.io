Unit 1

1) Characteristics of System

  • Organization: Structure and order that arranges components to achieve objectives.
  • Interaction: Manner in which components work together.
  • Interdependence: Subsystems depend on each other for functionality.
  • Integration: Unifies components into a cohesive whole.
  • Central Objective: Defined purpose or goal.

2) Elements of a System

  • Inputs and Outputs: Data entering and leaving the system.
  • Processor(s): Transform input into output.
  • Control: Decision-making components managing system behavior.
  • Feedback: Helps maintain system balance; can be positive or negative.
  • Environment: External factors influencing the system.
  • Boundaries and Interface: Define the scope and interaction with other systems.

3) Concept of System Analyst

Definition:

A system analyst is a professional with technical and interpersonal skills who guides system development to align with organizational goals.

Roles:

  • Understand user requirements using fact-finding techniques.
  • Prioritize and document requirements.
  • Suggest and implement flexible solutions.
  • Provide detailed specifications for users and programmers.
  • Ensure modular system design.
  • Facilitate system evaluation and updates.

Attributes:

  • Interpersonal Skills: Communication, leadership, and motivation.
  • Analytical Skills: Problem-solving, trade-off analysis, and curiosity.
  • Management Skills: Project management, change/risk management.
  • Technical Skills: Knowledge of computers, design tools, and emerging technologies.

4) Concept of Application Development Portfolio (ADP)

  • Definition: ADP refers to a set of proposed or approved projects under development that focus on:
    • Solving problems (e.g., reducing data entry errors).
    • Capitalizing on opportunities (e.g., offering discounts to frequent customers).
    • Responding to directives (e.g., legal compliance).

5) 5 C’s

  • Capability: Speed, volume processing, and information retrieval.
  • Control: Accuracy, consistency, and security.
  • Communication: Faster information flow and integration of business areas.
  • Cost: Cost monitoring and reduction.
  • Competitiveness: Enhancing customer relationships, locking out competitors, and enabling product development.
