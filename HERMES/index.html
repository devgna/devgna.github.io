Motivation

The Edge Explosion

IoT/Edge devices are critical infrastructure (Smart Cities, Industry 4.0).

Problem: Massive attack surface, but devices have limited CPU/Power.

The Security Gap

Traditional NIDS (Snort) are too heavy (require big CPUs).

Cloud analysis adds >100ms latency, unacceptable for real-time control.

Core Question: "How do we secure low-power ARM devices against Zero-Day attacks without draining their battery?"

Problem Statement

Resource Constraints

Edge nodes (Raspberry Pi 5) have limited thermal envelopes (5W) and shared RAM.

Static Signatures Fail

Rule-based systems cannot detect polymorphic or Zero-Day attacks common in IoT botnets.

AI Overhead

Standard Deep Learning models (ResNet/VGG) are too heavy for ARM inference (>30% packet loss).

Why Our Method Works (Theoretical)

1. The 80/20 Rule of Traffic

Network traffic follows a Pareto distribution. 80% of packets are benign or known attacks.

Strategy: Use a cheap Rule Filter (O(1) complexity) for the 80%, reserving the expensive ML (O(n)) only for the ambiguous 20%.

2. Quantization Efficiency

We convert 32-bit floats to 8-bit integers (INT8).

  • Reduces model size by 75%.
  • Uses ARM NEON SIMD instructions for faster math.

3. Robustness

Rules act as a "sanitizer," blocking obvious adversarial perturbations before they fool the AI.

Key Contributions

1. Hybrid Architecture

Dual-stage design tailoring NIDS for Edge constraints.

2. ARM Optimization

Demonstrated INT8 quantized ONNX inference on Raspberry Pi.

3. Energy Efficiency

Achieved 67% energy reduction vs x86 solutions.

4. Zero-Day Resilience

Validated 94.7% detection of unseen attacks.

Conclusion & Future Scope

Conclusion

HERMES proves that high-security does not require high-power hardware. By tailoring hybrid AI/ML specifically for ARM architecture, we have enabled robust, scalable protection for the next generation of Edge IoT networks.

Future Directions

  • Federated Learning: Collaborative model updates across edge clusters for privacy.
  • Encrypted Traffic Analysis: Inspecting TLS 1.3 flows without decryption (Fingerprinting).
  • Dynamic Load Shedding: Adaptive sampling during extreme DDoS events.
